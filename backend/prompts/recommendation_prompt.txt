## METRIC DEFINITIONS (for your diagnosis)
- faithfulness: Is the answer grounded in the retrieved context? Low score = hallucination.
- context_precision: Is the retrieved context relevant to the question? Low score = noisy/irrelevant retrieval.
- context_recall: Does the retrieved context contain what is needed to answer? Low score = missing information in retrieval.
- answer_relevancy: Does the answer directly address the question? Low score = off-topic or evasive answer.
- answer_correctness: Is the answer factually correct relative to ground truth? Low score = factual errors or omissions.
- rqs: Composite quality score. Low score = systemic issue across multiple dimensions.

## DIAGNOSTIC PRIORITY
Apply this priority order when multiple metrics are failing:
1. faithfulness — hallucination is the highest-risk failure; fix grounding first.
2. context_recall — if key information is not retrieved, no amount of generation tuning helps.
3. context_precision — noisy context degrades all downstream metrics.
4. answer_correctness — factual errors directly impact user trust.
5. answer_relevancy — off-topic answers suggest prompt or routing issues.
